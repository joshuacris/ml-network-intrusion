\chapter{Model Comparison Study}
\begin{spacing}{1.0}
\small

This chapter compares four supervised classifiers for binary network intrusion
detection on the same \textit{Normal} vs.\ \textit{Attack} task:
logistic regression, multi-layer perceptron (MLP), random forest, and
XGBoost. The goal is to compare an interpretable linear baseline, a neural
baseline, and two tree-based ensemble methods under a common evaluation and
tuning pipeline.

\section{Evaluation Setup}
\textbf{Protocol.} All models were trained on the same processed training split
and evaluated on the same held-out test split, with 3-fold cross-validation on
the training data for model selection. \textbf{Metrics.} ROC-AUC was used for
cross-validation because it measures ranking quality without fixing a decision
threshold. Precision, recall, and F1 were then reported on the test set because
intrusion detection must balance false alarms against missed attacks.
\textbf{Tuning.} Each model was tuned with RandomizedSearchCV using search
spaces broad enough to cover simpler and more expressive settings without
becoming computationally excessive. \textbf{Threshold analysis.} After fitting
the best cross-validated model, thresholds from 0.01 to 0.99 were swept and
the threshold maximizing attack-class F1 was selected. For logistic regression,
random forest, and XGBoost, the tuned predictions were also joined back to the
raw attack\_cat labels to compare attack-family difficulty.

All four models improved noticeably after threshold tuning, showing that the
default threshold of 0.50 is too conservative for this intrusion-detection
setting. Logistic regression improved from 0.7899 to 0.9080, random forest
from 0.8471 to 0.9091, and XGBoost from 0.8466 to 0.9154. This is direct
evidence that the hyperparameter and threshold choices were reasonable: the
selected configurations were non-default and the threshold sweep produced
better operating points for attack detection.

The tree-based models achieved the strongest ROC-AUC values, showing that the
intrusion features contain important nonlinear structure. XGBoost is the final
selected model because it achieved the best cross-validated ROC-AUC (0.9897),
the best test ROC-AUC (0.9761), and the best tuned F1 (0.9154). Random forest
was close behind and was especially strong on raw attack families, while
logistic regression remained the easiest model to interpret. MLP achieved a
test ROC-AUC of 0.9549 and a CV ROC-AUC of 0.9508, the lowest among all four
models. At the tuned threshold, logistic regression was weakest on
\textit{Fuzzers} (0.9591 recall), random forest was weakest on
\textit{Analysis} and \textit{Fuzzers} (both 0.8614), XGBoost improved
\textit{Fuzzers} to 0.9648 while keeping \textit{Analysis} as its hardest
group at 0.8645, and MLP struggled most on \textit{Fuzzers} at only 0.4475
recall — the lowest Fuzzers recall across all models.
