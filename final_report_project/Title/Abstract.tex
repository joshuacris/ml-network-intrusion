\begin{center}
{\Large \textbf{ABSTRACT}}
\end{center}
\small
\begin{spacing}{1.0}

This project compares four supervised models for binary network intrusion
detection on the UNSW-NB15 dataset: Logistic Regression, Multi-Layer
Perceptron (MLP), Random Forest, and XGBoost. After duplicate removal,
grouping rare categorical values, applying log transformations to 14 highly
skewed numerical features, and building model-specific tabular representations
with 58 predictors, we trained on 107{,}740 flows and evaluated on a held-out
test set of 55{,}945 flows. Hyperparameters were tuned with RandomizedSearchCV
using 3-fold cross-validation and ROC-AUC, and final classification thresholds
were chosen by sweeping from 0.01 to 0.99 to maximize attack-class F1. All
models improved after threshold tuning: Logistic Regression reached a tuned F1
of 0.7811, MLP 0.8571, Random Forest 0.8784, and XGBoost 0.8680. XGBoost
achieved the best CV ROC-AUC among tree-based models (0.9601) and is selected
as the final model; Random Forest achieved the highest test ROC-AUC (0.9729)
and tuned F1 (0.8784). Per-attack-category analysis showed that Fuzzers was
the hardest attack family across all models, while Worms, Generic, and Backdoor
were detected with near-perfect recall by the tree-based models. Overall, the
comparison shows that nonlinear ensemble methods are best suited to this tabular
intrusion-detection task, and XGBoost provides the best balance of detection
quality and generalization.
\end{spacing}
